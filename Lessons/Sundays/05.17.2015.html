From the beginning of time, we know there has been a heaven. We know this because God created it. Genesis 1:1 says "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth." Notice that in this verse that it says "heaven" and not "heavens." Many Bible versions, in order to match the concept of multiple heavens, make this into "heavens." This is why I prefer the King James Version, because it sticks to the original translations. You do not need to modify this word if you pay attention to what the chapter says. You must notice that Genesis 1 is in chronological order, meaning 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, etc. Straight off the Bible gets right into the action. Genesis 1:1 isn't a topic sentence, per se, but is the first step God took in creating all that would ever exist.

Because the Bible says this, I automatically will tell you that it is true. What is says is true and cannot tell a lie, because it is God's Word. Heaven is real and so is Hell. Many people will try to disprove that Hell really existed. They will tell you it is just symbolism for "death" and the "grave." Well, I tell you that is false. Hell is real. If the reward of salvation is eternal life in paradise, what is the punishment of not obtaining that salvation? It is eternal life in Hell! Now, if you remember from our lesson on Rightly Dividing, there is what is called the Kingdom Dispensation. If you don't remember or did not know of this lesson, let's take a crash course real quick. There are 7 major dispensations in the Bible (dispensation = time period in which God handles humanity in a specific way) which are meant to be divided. We are currently in the Dispensation of Grace (Acts 9-Philemon). The Kingdom Dispensation, which entails Hebrews-Revelation, is the last dispensation in the Bible. This dispensation takes place after the Rapture of the church. The Rapture is when Jesus comes back to take all who believed to Heaven.

The Kingdom Dispensation was not meant for us, the Body of Christ, but for the Nation of Israel after we are gone and in paradise, because Israel is still God's chosen nation and still wants to deal with them and give them an opportunity for salvation even though they have turned their backs on Him so many different times. We must understand that this is a future time and not now.

Because we know that the "Kingdom Dispensation" is meant for the nation of Israel and not the Body of Christ, we must also now understand that this is not Heaven. The Kingdom is not Heaven! By referring to what it is called, we can assume that this dispensation will most likely include the "kingdom." How can the kingdom be Heaven and we be gone and still be here on earth with the Israelis? It can't. We now know that the Kingdom refers to the kingdom that God will establish here on earth after the tribulation (as the scripture says. Do also know that the tribulation is in the same dispensation. The tribulation happens after the Rapture). Yet, people will try to dispute my claim here and quote this verse from John (being John 18:36) - "Jesus answered, My kingdom is not of this world: if my kingdom were of this world, then would my servants fight, that I should not be delivered to the Jews: but now is my kingdom not from hence." People will say it won't be in this world because of this verse, but I say they didn't read the verse correctly.

As believers we are told not to be of this world. We are walking in it, but are not of this world. What does this mean? It isn't of this world, it won't be of the world. It won't have the sinful lusts and desires that we know the world has.

Let's continue talking about Heaven now, enough with the kingdom. Heaven is where God is making each of us our own special place for all who believe. Heaven is special because it will not have any sorrow, pain, guilt, sin, etc. It will be perfect. Let's look at Revelation Chapter 21, (the second-to-last chapter of the Bible).

Revelation 21:1-8
